Project Name: Sathkara


-August-

The "Sathkaara" initiative focused on mental health and well-being for secondary school parents. Dr. Bhathiya Dasanayake, a renowned psychologist, worked with the initiative to educate parents about early childhood psychological and physical growth. Parents were educated on neuroplasticity, parenting, autism, ADHD, screen addiction, intelligence, empathy, and child personality. A questionnaire was given to parents to evaluate their children's development. The initiative was successful, and the project chairman requested more sessions. The second program, conducted at Puttalama Manaweriya Junior School, aimed to educate students about personality development, adolescence, sexuality issues, and childhood development, fostering self-awareness and understanding of emotions, behaviors, and personal identity.


-September-

The "Sathkara_September" initiative focused on mental health and well-being for secondary school parents at MaPa/Koth/ Widyakara Adarsha Primary School. Dr. Bhathiya Dasanayake, a renowned psychiatrist, provided invaluable insights into early childhood development. The initiative educated parents about recognizing and understanding their children's unique talents, neuroplasticity, effective parenting, Autism and ADHD, screen addiction, intelligence, empathy, and personality formation. Parents were provided with a questionnaire to self-reflect and understand their children. The initiative was well-received by the parents, who expressed gratitude for the initiative and requested another session.


-October-

The "Sathkara_October" initiative focused on "Mental Health and Well-Being" at Bapa/ Gam/ Sri Bodhi Primary School, Sri Bodhi Para, Gampaha. The initiative, led by renowned psychiatrist Dr. Bhathiya Dasanayake, educated parents of Grade 4 and 5 students about the importance of early childhood development. Parents learned about neuroplasticity, effective parenting, Autism and ADHD, screen addiction, intelligence, empathy, and personality formation. Parents were also given a questionnaire to deepen their understanding of their children. The initiative received heartfelt gratitude from the assembly and the principal requested another session to further highlight its impact.


-November-

The "Sathkara_November" project, focused on mental health and well-being, was initiated at Ra/Bala/Urawala College, Balangoda, involving students from Grade 9 to 11 and their parents. Dr. Bhathiya Dasanayake, a renowned psychiatrist, was partnered with, and the program was communicated to students and parents. The program involved two phases: educating parents about stress reduction and screen addiction and educating students from Grade 9 to 11 about personality development, adolescence, and sexuality issues. Parents learned about early childhood psychological and physical development, recognizing their children's unique abilities, and navigating adolescence with confidence. A questionnaire was given to parents to evaluate their children. The entire gathering expressed gratitude for the initiative.


-December-

The "Sathkara_December" project focuses on mental health and well-being, targeting students in grades 9-11 and their parents. The project was initiated at Wp/Mt/Halwala C.W.W. Kannangara Maha Vidyalaya, Kaluthara, in collaboration with Dr. Bhathiya Dasanayake, an early childhood development specialist. The school's bulletin board was used to inform students about Dr. Dasanayake's involvement and the program's significance, while parents were notified through their children. The project unfolded in two key phases. Firstly, a seminar for parents focused on awareness sessions addressing stress reduction and screen addiction in children. In the second phase, a separate seminar for students in grades 9 to 11 covered topics such as personality development, adolescence, sexuality issues, and childhood development. The resource person emphasized the importance of understanding early childhood psychological and physical development and recognizing children's unique abilities and preferences. They also emphasized the challenges of adolescence, fostering healthy self-identities and relationships, and equipping adolescents with emotional understanding, informed decision-making, and empathy


-January-

The "Sathkara_January" initiative focused on mental health and well-being for secondary school students. Dr. Bhathiya Dasanayake, a renowned psychiatrist, provided expert insights. The project involved discussions with the school principal and students, highlighting the importance of understanding children's psychological and physical development. Parents learned about neuroplasticity, effective parenting, autism, ADHD, screen addiction risks, multiple intelligences, empathy, and personality development. Parents also received a questionnaire to reflect on themselves and gain a better understanding of their children. The project was praised by the school's principal.
